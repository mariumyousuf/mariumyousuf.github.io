During graduate school, also teaching > active learning. active learning environment and how they differ from the traditional lecture-based classroom settings. I hold a great fascination with the work of Carol Dweck, who introduced these two categories of fixed and growth mindset. I grew up with a strict hold on a fixed outlook especially in a school environment (grades received, approach to studying, etc.). I know from my experience it can be difficult to train your mind to think differently and essentially relearn to be an entirely different person. Viewing the learning world with a lense of fixed mindset often led to quick discouragement with lower grades, while after adapting a growth mindset I realized how it highlights the scope of learning together with the progress made so far. It was not until my sophomore year in college that I started to recognize the importance of possessing a growth mindset and the positive attitude it nurtures towards learning. I firmly believe that having a growth mindset is crucial for the honest and long-term success of all students, especially those in STEM fields. Effects of having a growth mindset incorporated into the discussions of active learning really convinced me of the importance of developing it in students.
